# Ballroom: Scene Report

## Incident

- **Time**: 20:00:00
- **Event**: Ballroom lights fail for ~30 seconds
- **Time**: 20:00:30
- **Event**: Lights restore; diamond missing

## People Present In Ballroom (at 19:58 to 20:02)

- Lord Blackwood (host)
- Lady Violet
- Professor Elm
- Miss Scarlett (insurance)
- Mr. Green (museum curator)
- Two uniformed security guards (not suspects)

## Display Case Condition (post-incident)

- The display case is **closed, locked, and undamaged**.
- No pry marks; no broken hinges; no shattered glass.

## What Is In The Case

After guards opened the case for inspection, it contained:

- A **glass replica** shaped like the Heart of the Empire
- A small paper tag under the replica reading: `WS-02 / Batch 14`

## Physical Constraint

The Heart of the Empire was displayed in its ceremonial mount.

- The mount is a rigid presentation piece (approx. the size of a large grapefruit).
- Ballroom security policy forbade bags/backpacks; guests wore formal attire.
- Guards performed an immediate line-of-sight check of guests at 20:01.

(Conclusion: the item was not plausibly pocketed by a guest without being noticed.)

## Witness Note

Miss Scarlett states she saw Mr. Green behind the velvet rope near the case at **19:59** saying he was "checking glare on the glass".

## Guard Statement

Guard Sgt. Phelps reports:

- At approximately **19:58:45**, Mr. Green asked to step behind the velvet rope to "fix a reflection".
- Sgt. Phelps watched Mr. Green unlock the case, open it briefly, then close and re-lock it.
- No other guest crossed the velvet rope during that minute.
