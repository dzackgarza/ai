# The Case of the Missing Diamond - Solution

## The Answer

**Mr. Green** stole the diamond.

## How It Was Done

Mr. Green is the museum curator and has worked there for 20 years. He had access to the museum's storage rooms and security systems.

**The method:**

1. Mr. Green knew the diamond's case lock mechanism inside and out
2. He had a duplicate key (museum curators often have emergency access)
3. During the blackout, he opened the case from the back (the case had a rear access panel that was hidden from view)
4. He removed the diamond and replaced it with a perfect glass replica he had prepared earlier
5. He relocked the case from the back

## Why Others Are Eliminated

- **Professor Elm**: Had no physical access to the case's lock or back panel
- **Lady Violet**: No access to duplicate keys or security knowledge
- **Miss Scarlett**: No access to museum storage or duplicate keys
- **Lord Blackwood**: The owner - no motive to steal his own diamond
- **Detective Holmes**: Arrived after the crime

## Where Is the Diamond Now?

Mr. Green hid it in the museum's secure storage vault, where he has 24/7 access. He planned to retrieve it later when suspicion had died down.

## The Logic Path

1. **Size constraint** eliminates on-person theft
2. **Lock intact** eliminates forced entry
3. **Only someone with** museum access and case knowledge could do it
4. **Only Mr. Green** fits all criteria
