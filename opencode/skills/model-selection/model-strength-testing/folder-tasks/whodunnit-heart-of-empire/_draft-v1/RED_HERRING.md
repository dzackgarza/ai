# Red Herring Analysis

## Professor Elm - The Obvious Suspect

**Why he seems guilty:**

- Argued with Lord Blackwood about ownership
- Had notebook with diamond history
- Historian with motive to return diamond to museum

**Why he's innocent:**

- No access to case lock mechanism
- No duplicate keys
- No storage room access
- No glass replica making skills

## Lady Violet - The Desperate Suspect

**Why she seems guilty:**

- Gambling debts
- Open jewelry box (searched)
- Diary entries about wanting diamond gone

**Why she's innocent:**

- No access to case
- No duplicate keys
- No storage room access
- No glass replica skills

## Lord Blackwood - The Owner Suspect

**Why he seems guilty:**

- Financial troubles
- Insurance policy changes
- Could sell diamond to pay debts

**Why he's innocent:**

- No need to steal own diamond
- No access to storage vault
- No glass replica skills

## Detective Holmes - The Outsider Suspect

**Why he seems suspicious:**

- Arrived after crime
- Investigating role
- Could plant evidence

**Why he's innocent:**

- Arrived after crime occurred
- No access to case or storage
- No motive

## Mr. Green - The Real Culprit

**Why he's guilty:**

- Museum curator with case knowledge
- Storage room access
- Duplicate key access
- Glass replica skills
- Opportunity during blackout

**The perfect crime:** Used his position to commit and cover up the theft.
