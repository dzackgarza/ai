# The Case of the Missing Diamond - Full Details

## The Crime Scene

**Location**: Grand Ballroom, Blackwood Estate
**Time**: 8:00 PM, February 25, 2026
**Victim**: "Heart of the Empire" diamond
**Value**: $10 million

## The Suspects

1. **Lord Blackwood** - 58, diamond owner, wealthy businessman
2. **Lady Violet** - 45, Lord Blackwood's wife, socialite
3. **Professor Elm** - 62, historian, visiting scholar
4. **Miss Scarlett** - 35, insurance agent, diamond expert
5. **Mr. Green** - 48, museum curator, security expert
6. **Detective Holmes** - 40, investigator, arrived after crime

## Timeline

- **7:30 PM**: Miss Scarlett examines diamond, confirms authenticity
- **7:45 PM**: Lord Blackwood argues with Professor Elm about ownership
- **7:55 PM**: Lady Violet seen near display case
- **8:00 PM**: Lights go out for 30 seconds
- **8:00:30 PM**: Lights return, diamond gone
- **8:10 PM**: Detective Holmes arrives

## Physical Evidence

- Display case: Locked, undamaged, no forced entry
- Diamond size: 10 carats, too large for pockets
- Security: No alarms triggered
- Power: Brief blackout, no system failures

## The Perfect Crime

Mr. Green's method required:

1. Knowledge of case construction
2. Access to duplicate keys
3. Ability to work in darkness
4. Access to glass replica
5. Knowledge of storage vault security

Only the museum curator possessed all these capabilities.
